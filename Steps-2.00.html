Problem Statement 2

In 2010, there were 161,401 arrests in the city of Los Angeles and 208,842 reported crimes.  In the 1990s and 2000s, California led the way ramping up mass incarceration with harsh three strikes and truth-in-sentencing laws and as prosecutors pushed sentences to the max. In the last decade, Californians have repeatedly voted to reject over-incarceration as a failed pathway to safety. Proposition 47 and proposition 57 were passed to reform the criminal justice system in California. Will this be enough to decrease the number of people that are being arrested?
